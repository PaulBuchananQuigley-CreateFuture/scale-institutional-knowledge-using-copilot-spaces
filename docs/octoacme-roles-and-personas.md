# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user flows, wireframes, and interaction designs that ensure software meets user needs. They bridge the gap between customer insight and technical implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Design user flows, wireframes, and prototypes
- Conduct or coordinate usability testing and user research
- Collaborate with Product Managers and Developers to translate requirements into intuitive interfaces
- Review features before release to validate usability and accessibility

### Goals
- Deliver experiences that are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user needs
- Reduce rework caused by poor UX decisions made late in the cycle
- Establish and maintain a shared design system

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Developers and PdMs
- Usability test findings shared with the wider team
- Handoff artefacts (e.g., Figma links, annotation docs) attached to issues or PRs

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather, document, and communicate requirements. They act as a liaison between stakeholders and the delivery team to ensure business needs are translated into clear, actionable work.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business and functional requirements
- Translate stakeholder needs into user stories, acceptance criteria, and process flows
- Facilitate requirements workshops and sign-off sessions
- Validate that delivered features meet stated business objectives

### Goals
- Ensure the delivery team has clear, unambiguous requirements before work begins
- Reduce re-work caused by misunderstood or incomplete specifications
- Maintain a traceable link between business goals and delivered features

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Written user stories and acceptance criteria added to the backlog
- Sign-off sessions with stakeholders at key milestones

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers design and maintain the infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, and deployment automation that enable reliable, repeatable releases.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Manage cloud or on-premise infrastructure as code
- Monitor system health, alerts, and on-call processes
- Collaborate with Developers on deployment strategies and environment parity
- Support incident response for infrastructure-related issues

### Goals
- Maximise deployment frequency while minimising failure rate and recovery time
- Eliminate manual, error-prone deployment steps
- Maintain observable, resilient production systems

### Typical Communication
- Infrastructure change reviews with Developers and Security Lead
- Deployment notes and runbooks attached to releases
- On-call handover and incident post-mortems

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
The Scrum Master facilitates agile ceremonies, coaches the team on agile principles, and removes impediments that slow delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate standups, sprint planning, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Coach the team on agile values and practices
- Identify and remove blockers that the team cannot resolve on their own
- Protect the team from unplanned work and scope creep during a sprint
- Coordinate with the Project Manager and Product Manager on process health

### Goals
- Maintain a high-performing, self-organising team
- Continuously improve team velocity and delivery predictability
- Foster a culture of transparency and continuous improvement

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Impediment log shared with PM and PdM
- Retrospective action-item follow-up

---

## Security Lead

### Role Summary
The Security Lead reviews features and changes for security risks, directs security best practices, and ensures compliance obligations are met throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Review architecture and design decisions for security implications
- Conduct or coordinate threat modelling and security testing
- Define and maintain security standards, tooling, and compliance requirements
- Engage during planning, execution, and release phases to flag and mitigate risk
- Lead the response to security incidents and ensure post-incident review

### Goals
- Prevent security vulnerabilities from reaching production
- Embed security practices early in the development lifecycle ("shift left")
- Ensure the team meets regulatory and compliance obligations

### Typical Communication
- Security review comments on PRs and design docs
- Risk register entries for security-related risks
- Security incident notifications and post-incident reports

---

## Executive Sponsor

### Role Summary
The Executive Sponsor provides strategic oversight, secures resources, and resolves escalated issues that are beyond the authority of the Project or Product Manager.

### Responsibilities
- Champion the project at the leadership level and secure budget and headcount
- Provide direction at key project gates (initiation, major milestones, release)
- Help resolve escalated blockers, conflicts, or strategic trade-offs
- Ensure the project remains aligned with organisational goals
- Communicate project outcomes to senior leadership

### Goals
- Ensure the project delivers measurable business value
- Remove organisational barriers that block progress
- Maintain executive and stakeholder confidence in the initiative

### Typical Communication
- Monthly or milestone-based project briefings from the PM
- Gate reviews at initiation, mid-point, and release
- Ad-hoc escalation meetings when critical decisions are needed
